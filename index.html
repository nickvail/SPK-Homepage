Sour. Sweet. Gone.

The Candy

Sour Patch Kids are small, flavoured candies that start in your mouth sour, but then turn sweet. The candies are covered in tartaric and citric acid, which reacts with the saliva in your mouth to create their iconic sour taste. After this coating is gone, you get to experience the sweetness on the inside. Originally, there were only four flavours one would het in a bag: Lemon (yellow), orange (peach), green (lime), and red (cherry). In 2014, the blue raspberry flavour was added, which went on to become the most popular flavour.

The History

Originally named 'Mars Men', Sour Patch Kids were created by Frank Galatolie of Jaret International in the early 1970s. The candy was first manufactured in Hamilton, Ontario by Cadbury and the Smeera Blyton Licorice Company of Sweden. The two companies created the Allen Candy Company to produce them. The Allen Candy Company was then merged with Malaco Licorice Company, creating M & A Candy Company. The original name was to capitalize off the sci-fi boom in the '70s. The name was later switched to capitalize off the popularity of the Cabbage Patch Kids in the '80s. In the late '90s Cadbury-Adams acquired the candy, and then later sold it to Mondolez International Group, who has owned it since.

The Game

In December 2011, Capcom published World Gone Sour, a side-scrolling platform game developed by Canadian studio Playbrains. The game follows a green Sour Patch Kid in a quest to be eaten, and is rated 'T' for 'Teen'. Initially released on Windows to mediocre reviews, it was added to the Playstation Network and the Xbox Live Arcade five months later in April 2012. The game was delisted from said plaforms in August 2015.
